### 2. DETAILED EXPLANATION: How Compliance Works in Practice

Industrial compliance is a multi-layered discipline. To understand "what is compliance" in 2026, one must look at the intersection of three distinct pillars: Statutory Compliance, Regulatory Compliance, and Preventive Maintenance Compliance (PMC).

#### The Three Pillars of Industrial Compliance

1.  **Statutory Compliance:** These are the laws enacted by government bodies. In the United States, this includes **OSHA 1910 (General Industry)** standards, which cover everything from Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) procedures to machine guarding. Failure to comply results in heavy fines and potential plant shutdowns.
2.  **Regulatory Compliance:** These are the specific rules set by agencies like the FDA or EPA. For example, **21 CFR Part 11** governs electronic records and signatures in the food and pharmaceutical sectors, while **Scope 3 Emissions** reporting is now a mandatory requirement for many manufacturers aiming for ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) targets.
3.  **Preventive Maintenance Compliance (PMC):** This is an internal metric that measures how many scheduled maintenance tasks were completed on time. A high PMC score is the leading indicator of a healthy plant. [Preventive maintenance](/products/prevent) is the proactive arm of compliance; if you aren't maintaining your assets, you are inherently out of compliance with safety and quality standards.

#### Common Compliance Pitfalls
Even with the best intentions, many maintenance departments fall into "compliance traps" that leave them vulnerable during audits:
*   **The "Pencil Whipping" Trap:** This occurs when technicians mark inspections as "complete" without actually performing the work, often due to time pressure. Factory AI solves this by requiring sensor-validated proof of operation or location-based check-ins.
*   **Siloed Data:** Safety records live in one filing cabinet, while maintenance logs live in another. When an auditor asks for the maintenance history of a failed safety valve, the delay in connecting these dots can result in a non-compliance citation.
*   **Ignoring "Ghost Assets":** These are secondary assets (like backup generators or auxiliary pumps) that aren't in the primary CMMS but are still subject to regulatory oversight. A comprehensive [asset management](/features/asset-management) strategy must account for every serialized component in the facility.

#### Edge Case: The "What If" of Asset Modification
A common compliance headache occurs when a machine is modified to improve throughput. Does the original manufacturer’s maintenance schedule still apply? In a compliant environment, any modification triggers a "Management of Change" (MOC) workflow. Factory AI automates this by flagging modified assets and prompting the maintenance manager to update the [PM procedures](/features/pm-procedures) to reflect the new operating parameters, ensuring the plant doesn't drift out of regulatory alignment.

#### Real-World Scenario: The Food & Beverage Plant
Consider a mid-sized F&B facility governed by **Current Good Manufacturing Practices (cGMP)**. Compliance here isn't just about clean floors; it’s about proving that the refrigeration units were maintained according to manufacturer specifications to prevent bacterial growth. 

Using **Factory AI**, the plant automates this proof. Sensors on the compressors (integrated via [predictive maintenance for compressors](/solutions/predictive-maintenance-compressors)) feed data into the CMMS. If a vibration pattern deviates from the norm, Factory AI triggers a [work order](/features/work-order-software) automatically. This creates a digital paper trail that satisfies an FDA auditor instantly, moving the plant from "defensive" compliance to "proactive" profit protection.

#### Technical Standards to Know
*   **ISO 55000 / 55001:** The international standard for asset management. It requires organizations to demonstrate a lifecycle approach to their machinery.
*   **ISO 9001:** The gold standard for quality management systems (QMS).
*   **EHS (Environmental Health and Safety):** A framework that ensures the plant does not harm its workers or the surrounding environment.

Compliance is no longer a static document in a binder; it is a dynamic, data-driven state of being. By utilizing [manufacturing AI software](/solutions/manufacturing-ai-software), companies can ensure that their "as-built" reality matches their "as-required" documentation.
