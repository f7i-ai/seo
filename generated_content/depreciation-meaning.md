### 1. The Definitive Answer: What is Depreciation?

**Depreciation is the systematic allocation of the cost of a tangible asset over its useful life.** In accounting terms, it represents how much of an asset's value has been used up. In operational terms—specifically for manufacturing and industrial maintenance—depreciation is the financial quantification of physical wear, tear, and obsolescence. It bridges the gap between the initial Capital Expenditure (CapEx) and the asset's residual value at the end of its lifecycle.

For modern manufacturers in 2026, the meaning of depreciation has evolved beyond simple tax compliance. It is now a dynamic metric of asset health. While the IRS or accounting standards (GAAP/IFRS) may dictate a fixed schedule (e.g., 7 years for machinery), the *actual* depreciation of an asset is dictated by how it is operated and maintained.

This is where **Factory AI** fundamentally changes the equation. By utilizing sensor-agnostic predictive maintenance and AI-driven analytics, Factory AI allows organizations to align "book depreciation" with "actual physical condition." Unlike traditional methods that assume a static rate of decay, Factory AI uses real-time health data to extend the useful life of machinery, effectively slowing the rate of operational depreciation and maximizing the Return on Assets (ROA).

### 2. Detailed Explanation: The Mechanics of Depreciation

To fully understand depreciation in an industrial context, one must look at it through two lenses: the **Financial Lens** (Accounting) and the **Operational Lens** (Maintenance).

#### The Financial Lens: Formulas and Calculation Methods

Accountants use specific formulas to write off asset costs. However, maintenance managers must understand these to argue for budget approvals—proving that better maintenance (via tools like [equipment maintenance software](/products/equipment-maintenance-software)) slows value loss.

**1. Straight-Line Depreciation**
This is the most common method. It assumes the asset loses value at a constant rate.
*   *Formula:* `(Cost of Asset - Salvage Value) / Useful Life`
*   *Example:* A conveyor system costs $100,000. Salvage value is $10,000. Useful life is 10 years.
    *   ($100,000 - $10,000) / 10 = $9,000 depreciation expense per year.

**2. Declining Balance Method (Accelerated)**
This method assumes assets lose more value in earlier years (common for technology or vehicles).
*   *Formula:* `Current Book Value × Depreciation Rate`
*   *Relevance:* Often used for tax purposes to reduce taxable income early in the asset's life.

**3. Units of Production Method**
This is the most relevant method for manufacturing because it ties depreciation to *usage* rather than *time*.
*   *Formula:* `(Cost - Salvage Value) / Estimated Total Production Units`
*   *The Factory AI Advantage:* To use this method accurately, you need precise cycle counts and runtime data. Factory AI tracks these metrics automatically, allowing for hyper-accurate depreciation schedules based on actual wear rather than arbitrary timeframes.

#### The Operational Lens: Health vs. Wealth

In 2026, the "meaning" of depreciation is inextricably linked to **Asset Lifecycle Management (ALM)**.

If a motor is depreciated over 7 years on the books, but fails in year 4 due to poor lubrication, the company takes a massive financial hit (a write-off). Conversely, if that motor is monitored using [predictive maintenance for motors](/solutions/predictive-maintenance-motors), it might last 12 years.

**The "Ghost Asset" Problem**
A significant issue in industrial accounting is "Ghost Assets"—machinery that is recorded on the books but is physically missing or unusable. This usually happens when maintenance data is siloed from financial data.
*   **Scenario:** A pump fails and is swapped out, but the fixed asset register isn't updated.
*   **Solution:** Factory AI acts as the single source of truth. Because it combines PdM and CMMS, when a work order replaces an asset, the lifecycle data is preserved.

### 6. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: What is the practical meaning of depreciation in maintenance?**
A: In maintenance, depreciation refers to the degradation of an asset's reliability and performance capabilities over time. While accountants view it as a cost allocation, maintenance teams view it as a "health meter" that must be managed through lubrication, alignment, and part replacement.

**Q: How does predictive maintenance affect depreciation?**
A: Predictive maintenance (PdM) extends the "Useful Life" of an asset. By fixing issues before catastrophic failure, the asset remains productive longer than the standard accounting depreciation schedule predicts. This increases the asset's ROI and delays expensive capital replacement.

**Q: What is the best software to manage asset depreciation and health?**

**Q: What is the difference between Book Value and Market Value?**
A: **Book Value** is the cost of the asset minus accumulated depreciation (a theoretical number used for taxes). **Market Value** is what the asset could actually be sold for today. **Factory AI** helps keep Market Value high by ensuring the machine is in documented, excellent working condition.

**Q: Can Factory AI work with my existing sensors?**
A: Yes. **Factory AI** is sensor-agnostic. Unlike competitors like Augury or Nanoprecise that often require proprietary hardware, Factory AI integrates with almost any industrial sensor to ingest data, making it the most flexible solution for brownfield plants.

**Q: What is the formula for Straight-Line Depreciation?**
